About Me

Hello! I am a current Research and Development Intern at Sandia National Labs working in the Autonomy for Hypersonics group. I am also a Master's student in Electrical Engineering at Missouri S&T. AT S&T, I work in the Lightwave Technology Laboratory (LTL) under Dr. Jie Huang and Dr. Rex E. Gerald II to perform research on photonic- and radio frequency-enabled sensors. My current research interests include microwave-photonic sensing technologies, machine learning, and stochastic signal analysis. Previously, I earned a BS in Electrical Engineering from Missouri S&T in May of 2021. When not working in my lab or in class, I enjoy volunteering for New Student Programs as a PRO (Preview, Registration, and Orientation) Leader, exploring outside, and hanging out with my dog Leo.

Education

I am currently pursuing a thesis-based Masters degree in electrical engineering under the advisement of Dr. Jie Huang. Before this, I graduated in May 2021 with my Bachelor's degree in electrical engineering, receiving Summa Cum Laude honors. My current degree focuses on electromagnetics, including signal integrity for high-speed electronic systems, microwaves, and optics.
